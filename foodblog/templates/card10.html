Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup sake
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 3 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • Sesame seeds and green onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Marinade:

    In a bowl, whisk together soy sauce, sake, mirin, brown sugar, honey, minced garlic, and grated ginger to create the teriyaki marinade.

  2. Marinate the Chicken:

    Cut the chicken thighs into bite-sized pieces. Place the chicken in a resealable plastic bag or a bowl and pour half of the teriyaki marinade over it. Seal the bag or cover the bowl, ensuring the chicken is well-coated. Marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes (or up to overnight for enhanced flavor).

  3. Cook the Chicken:

    Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade, allowing excess marinade to drip off. Add the chicken to the hot skillet and cook until browned on all sides and cooked through.

  4. Make the Teriyaki Sauce:

    In a small saucepan, combine the remaining teriyaki marinade with cornstarch and water. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens. This will be the teriyaki glaze for the chicken.

  5. Glaze and Serve:

    Once the chicken is cooked, pour the teriyaki glaze over it, ensuring each piece is coated evenly. Toss the chicken in the glaze until well-coated and heated through. Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions if desired.

Conclusion:

Cooking Tip:

Achieving the perfect teriyaki chicken involves balancing sweet, salty, and savory flavors. To enhance the taste, marinate the chicken for at least 30 minutes or, for better results, let it marinate in the refrigerator for a few hours. When grilling or pan-searing, baste the chicken with the teriyaki sauce throughout the cooking process for a rich glaze. For an extra burst of freshness, sprinkle sesame seeds and chopped green onions on the finished dish. Remember, patience is key – allowing the flavors to meld will result in a teriyaki chicken that's truly a culinary delight.
