Flavorful Thai Basil Chicken: A Quick and Easy Recipe for Weeknight Delight

Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red chili, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup Thai basil leaves, loosely packed
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Cooked jasmine rice, for serving

Instructions:


  1. Prepare the Ingredients:

    Begin by slicing the chicken into thin strips. Mince the garlic, chop the red chili, slice the bell pepper and onion, and gather the fresh Thai basil leaves. Having everything ready will make the cooking process smoother.

  2. Sauté the Chicken:

    In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped red chili, sautéing for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the sliced chicken and cook until it turns golden brown and is cooked through.

  3. Add Vegetables:

    Once the chicken is cooked, add the sliced bell pepper and onion to the skillet. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp, maintaining their vibrant colors.

  4. Season the Dish::

    In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and sugar. Pour this flavorful sauce over the chicken and vegetables, ensuring everything is well coated. Stir and let the ingredients meld together for another 2-3 minutes.

  5. Add Thai Basil:

    The pièce de résistance of this dish is the fresh Thai basil. Add the basil leaves to the skillet, gently stirring until they wilt and release their aromatic essence into the dish. This step should only take a minute or two to maintain the basil's vibrant green color and robust flavor.

  6. Serve:

    Once the Thai Basil Chicken is well-cooked and the flavors have melded beautifully, it's time to serve. Spoon the fragrant mixture over a bed of cooked jasmine rice and garnish with extra Thai basil leaves for an added touch.

Cooking Tip:

To intensify the aromatic flavors of your Thai Basil Chicken, try adding the fresh Thai basil leaves towards the end of the cooking process. This helps to preserve the herb's vibrant aroma and essence, ensuring that each bite bursts with the authentic taste of Thai cuisine. The brief exposure to heat allows the basil to infuse the dish without losing its freshness. Remember, it's the final touch that brings this dish to a whole new level of culinary delight!
